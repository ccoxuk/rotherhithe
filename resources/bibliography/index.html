    5.3 Bibliography

    Bibliography and Further Reading

    Project Management - Association for Project Management (2019). APM Body of Knowledge, 7th ed. - Project Management Institute (2021). A Guide to the Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK Guide), 7th ed. - Turner, J.R. (2016). The Handbook of Project-Based Management, 4th ed. McGraw-Hill.

    Theatre Buildings and Technical Design - Ham, Roderick (1987). Theatre Planning: Guidance for Design and Adaptation. Architectural Press. (Classic text, still relevant) - Izenour, George (1996). Theater Design, 2nd ed. Yale University Press. - Mackintosh, Iain (1993). Architecture, Actor and Audience. Routledge. - Strong, Judith (2010). Theatre Buildings: A Design Guide. Routledge. (Excellent modern resource)

    Site-Specific and Immersive Theatre - Lavender, Andy (2016). Performance in the Twenty-First Century: Theatres of Engagement. Routledge. - Machon, Josephine (2013). Immersive Theatres: Intimacy and Immediacy in Contemporary Performance. Palgrave. - Pearson, Mike (2010). Site-Specific Performance. Palgrave. - Wilkie, Fiona (2015). Performance, Transport and Mobility. Palgrave. (Includes promenade theatre)

    Planning, Development, and Regeneration - Carmona, Matthew et al. (2021). Public Places Urban Spaces: The Dimensions of Urban Design, 3rd ed. Routledge. - Cullingworth, Barry and Nadin, Vincent (2006). Town and Country Planning in the UK, 14th ed. Routledge. - Roberts, Peter and Sykes, Hugh (2016). Urban Regeneration, 2nd ed. SAGE.

    Arts Management and Cultural Policy - Arts Council England (2020). Let's Create: Strategy 2020-2030. Available: artscouncil.org.uk - Byrnes, William J. (2014). Management and the Arts, 5th ed. Focal Press. - Hewison, Robert (2014). Cultural Capital: The Rise and Fall of Creative Britain. Verso. - Selwood, Sara (ed.) (2001). The UK Cultural Sector. Policy Studies Institute.

    Community Engagement and Participation - Bishop, Claire (2012). Artificial Hells: Participatory Art and the Politics of Spectatorship. Verso. - Matarasso, François (1997). Use or Ornament? The Social Impact of Participation in the Arts. Comedia. (Influential study) - Skinner, Steve (2006). Effective Community Engagement. Communities and Local Government. Available online.

    Heritage and Conservation - Historic England (2016). Conservation Principles, Policies and Guidance. Available: historicengland.org.uk - Pendlebury, John (2009). Conservation in the Age of Consensus. Routledge. - Strike, James (1994). Architecture in Conservation: Managing Development at Historic Sites. Routledge.

    Health, Safety, and Risk Management - Health and Safety Executive. Managing Health and Safety in Construction (CDM Regulations 2015). Available: hse.gov.uk/pubns/priced/l153.pdf - HSE. Health and Safety in Roof Work. (Relevant for scaffold safety) - Hopkin, Paul (2018). Fundamentals of Risk Management, 5th ed. Kogan Page.

    Funding and Finance - Arts Council England. Guidance on Grants for the Arts Applications. Updated regularly; available online. - Joy, Annabel and Phelan, Chris (2020). Getting In: A Practical Guide to Arts Fundraising. Directory of Social Change. - Sahlman, William A. (1997). "How to Write a Great Business Plan", Harvard Business Review. (Principles apply to nonprofits)

    Legal Structures and Governance - Community Interest Companies Regulator. Information and Guidance Notes. Available: gov.uk/government/organisations/office-of-the-regulator-of-community-interest-companies - Charity Commission. The Essential Trustee: What You Need to Know. Available: gov.uk/government/publications/the-essential-trustee-what-you-need-to-know-cc3

    Case Studies and Reports - Battersea Arts Centre (2018). Scratch Hub Report: Lessons from 10 Years of Artist Development. Available: bac.org.uk - BOP Consulting (2017). World Cities Culture Report. worldcitiescultureforum.com (Includes London cultural infrastructure data) - Greater London Authority (2019). Culture and the Night-Time Economy. Available: london.gov.uk - Theatres Trust (2020). Theatres at Risk Register. theatrestrust.org.uk (Documents threatened venues)

    Relevant UK Legislation and Guidance - Town and Country Planning Act 1990 - Planning and Compulsory Purchase Act 2004 - Localism Act 2011 - National Planning Policy Framework (NPPF) 2021 (and any subsequent updates) - Building Regulations 2010 (England and Wales) - Building Act 1984 - Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015 - Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 - Licensing Act 2003 - Equality Act 2010 - Companies Act 2006 - Charities Act 2011 - Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Areas Act 1979 - Planning (Listed Buildings and Conservation Areas) Act 1990

    All legislation available at: legislation.gov.uk

    About the Author

    Craig Cox MCIPR

    Craig Cox MCIPR is a strategic communications and project-delivery adviser who specialises in civic infrastructure, cultural placemaking, and complex stakeholder programmes. A Chartered Institute of Public Relations member, he blends policy insight with practical delivery support for organisations that need to work quickly inside UK government systems.

    Current Roles - Director, navigategov.uk Ltd – a UK-registered public relations and communications company that publishes manuals, toolkits, and training for borough-scale cultural projects. - Director, The Better Growth Company Ltd – a boutique consultancy providing political-risk intelligence, growth planning, and executive coaching to public-interest ventures.

    Practice Focus - Translating regulatory, funding, and technical requirements for creative and community-led clients. - Leading feasibility, governance, and risk reviews for meanwhile-use assets, heritage structures, and transport-adjacent schemes. - Coaching leadership teams through stakeholder engagement, crisis messaging, and government relations.
