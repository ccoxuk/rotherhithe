    1.1 Executive Summary

    Executive Summary

    Status: Publication-Ready Guidance (v1.2) Epistemic Basis: UK Statute, Industry Best Practice (CDM 2015, PMBOK), and Applied Governance Frameworks.

    1. Strategic Purpose

    This manual provides a rigorous, end-to-end governance and delivery framework for establishing temporary or permanent theatre venues in complex urban environments. It is specifically calibrated for railway-adjacent sites in Rotherhithe, Southeast London, but its methodology is applicable to any high-constraint capital arts project.

    It transforms the "artistic vision" of a venue into a bankable, insurable, and legally compliant project structure. It is designed to satisfy the due diligence requirements of: * Statutory Bodies: Local Planning Authorities, Health & Safety Executive, Network Rail. * Funders: Arts Council England, NLHF, Social Investors. * Oversight: Boards of Trustees, Steering Groups, Insurers.

    2. Core Methodology

    The manual enforces a Three-Phase Project Lifecycle (Discovery → Implementation → Closure) to control risk and capital exposure. * Discovery: Low-cost, high-intellect phase to validate feasibility before significant spend. * Implementation: Rigorous project management of the build, licensing, and operation. * Closure: Structured demobilisation to protect reputation and capture learning.

    3. Risk and Governance Profile

    This is not merely a "how-to" guide; it is a risk management system. * Regulatory Compliance: Explicitly addresses CDM 2015, Licensing Act 2003, and GDPR. * Operational Safety: Mandates specific protocols for railway proximity (Asset Protection). * Financial Control: Requires sensitivity analysis and contingency planning (Low/Likely/High).

    4. Intended Audience and Usage

    • Executive Leadership (Board/Directors): Use Part One and Part Three to establish governance and strategy.
    • Project Managers/Producers: Use Part Two and Part Four (Templates) for daily execution.
    • Technical/Site Managers: Use Appendix 13.5 (Risk), 13.9 (H&S), and 13.11 (Rail) for operational safety.

    Critical Note: This document constitutes guidance, not professional advice. All technical, legal, and safety decisions must be ratified by qualified professionals (Structural Engineers, Solicitors, Principal Designers) appointed to the specific project.
